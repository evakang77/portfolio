Project Vision

Help Cornell students who are planning to live off-campus for the first time in Ithaca find housing more efficiently.


Design Process Overview

User Group

Cornell students who are planning to live off-campus for the first time in Ithaca(e.g. rising Sophomores, rising Juniors who have been living on campus, incoming transfer students, first-year graduate/doctoral students, international students).

Problem Space

  • Due to the user group’s lack of experience, they don’t know what to expect and are overwhelmed by the whole housing hunting process.
  • The user group doesn't know the timeline to look for housing (when it’s starting and when all houses are taken).
  • The user group can’t find a desirable place to live even after spending a long time researching.
  • The user group doesn’t know what’s important to check or to inquire about when touring houses/apartments (amenities included, equipment breakdown, etc.).

Enthusiasm

As many of us in Group 35 had really struggled when we were finding housing for the first time in Ithaca, we can thoroughly empathize with other fellow students in similar situations. As responsible Cornellians and designers ourselves, we are eager to use our skills and knowledge to help them complete their goals more efficiently and accurately.

Impact

  • It will help the users to be more confident and informed about the whole process.
  • It will help the users to find their ideal housing option faster.
  • It will save a lot of time for the users so that they can better concentrate on their school, work, and personal lives.

Solution Space

We started this part by collecting the existing solutions identified by every team member. We observe that there are both technical and non-technical solutions and we collected them into five categories.

The centralized housing listing sites lack a way for every student to quickly share an interested listing to their future roommates (right now students have to use the share button, if exists, to send the link to roommates externally, which is very troublesome and inefficient), and these sites don’t inform users how popular a listing is (i.e. how much potential competition they are facing).

Forum like reddit is a good resource to get information on landlord review by past tenants (an important criterion as reported by many interviewees), however, the information is limited and reddit is only one of the many tools students use during the process. All interviewees mentioned mostly other tools for house hunting.

The individual housing listing sites are only useful for students who trust a certain landlord company like Ithaca Renting Company, and prefer an apartment over a house.

The Search Engine and the Non-Tech solution spaces provide solutions that are either too “general” or too “specific” for the housing searching process. While Google and the brochure can in fact give students an overview of off-campus housings and the process of finding them, they lack detailed instructions and timeline for doing so.

None of the solutions provide students how many other people are interested––also begs for a solution that would allow them to make the most timely decisions.

As a team, we learned from the five solution spaces, while they have some flaws and missed opportunities. From there we started brainstorming on technical and non-technical aspects. After discussing within our group, we ruled out ideas that are either not needed based on our user or ideas that already exist as a major function of other platforms. Therefore, our house hunting app has the following four functionalities:

  1. Recommend the most suitable house/apartment to the user according to the preference form filled out by the user.
  2. Allow users to like listings and count the number of people who are interested to help users make decisions before housings are gone.
  3. Allow users to friend future roommates and share in-app information to keep everyone updated.
  4. Ithaca house hunting timeline to help users know the best time to start searching for housing; attend events based on the house hunting schedule.

Prototyping

Low-fidelity sketch

Used pen and paper to hand-sketch the primary screens of the app.

Takeaways:

  • Nice overview of the entire app and its functionalities
  • Definitely need to work on adding more details and allow users to interact with the prototype.

Interactive paper prototype (click to view)

Used pen, paper, and other tools to create paper prototype users can physically interact with.

Takeaways:

  • The illustrations are sometimes confusing
  • The user tend to expect more from the paper prototype, however, the appearance and content are both incomplete because what we are testing is the functionalities and the user flow.

High-fidelity prototype

Used Figma to create High-fidelity prototype users can get the real feeling as interacting with the fully developed app.

Takeaways:

  • If the icons are not used properly, they can cause a lot of confusion and are not user-friendly.
  • Users are more confident in navigating through tasks that they’ve done before on other existing apps
  • There might be some nuances between the designers' mind and the users' mind. So usability testing is very useful and necessary.
